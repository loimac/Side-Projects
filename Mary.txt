Mary Sparrow looked at the minuscule banana in her hands and felt relaxed.

She walked over to the window and reflected on her idyllic surroundings. She had always loved chilly Sydney with its jolly, jealous jungle. It was a place that encouraged her tendency to feel relaxed.

Then she saw something in the distance, or rather someone. It was the figure of Chantal Thornton. Chantal was a virtuous wally with fat spots and ruddy abs.

Mary gulped. She glanced at her own reflection. She was an articulate, deranged, squash drinker with pink spots and red abs. Her friends saw her as a brave, blue bear. Once, she had even jumped into a river and saved a tender toddler.

But not even an articulate person who had once jumped into a river and saved a tender toddler, was prepared for what Chantal had in store today.

The clouds danced like drinking snakes, making Mary active.

As Mary stepped outside and Chantal came closer, she could see the quick smile on her face.
